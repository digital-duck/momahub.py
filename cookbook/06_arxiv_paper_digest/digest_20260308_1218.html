Here is a structured digest of the paper: **1. Title & Core Claim** The paper, "Mamba: Linear-Time Sequence Modeling with Selective State Spaces," claims to contribute a new class of selective state space models that achieve Transformer-quality performance while scaling linearly in sequence length. **2. Problem Statement** The paper addresses the problem of inefficient sequence modeling architectures, particularly the Transformer architecture, which has fundamental drawbacks such as quadratic scaling and inability to model anything outside of a finite window. The authors argue that this inefficiency hinders the adoption of foundation models (FMs) for various applications. **3. Methodology** The authors propose a new class of selective state space models (SSMs) that improve on prior work by introducing a selection mechanism, which allows the model to filter out irrelevant information and remember relevant information indefinitely. They also design a hardware-aware algorithm to compute the model recurrently with a scan instead of convolution. **4. Key Results** The paper reports several key results, including: * Mamba achieves state-of-the-art performance across various modalities such as language, audio, and genomics. * The selective SSMs outperform prior SSMs on dense modalities such as language and genomics. * Mamba's performance improves with longer context up to million-length sequences. * The model enjoys fast inference (5× higher throughput than Transformers) and linear scaling in sequence length. **5. Limitations & Open Questions** The paper leaves unresolved the following limitations and open questions: * While Mamba outperforms prior SSMs, it is unclear whether this performance gain generalizes to all domains. * The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their hardware-aware algorithm. **6. Relevance** This paper is most relevant to researchers and practitioners working on sequence modeling architectures, particularly those interested in foundation models and their applications. **7. One-Line Summary** Mamba: A new class of selective state space models that achieve Transformer-quality performance while scaling linearly in sequence length, outperforming prior SSMs on dense modalities and enjoying fast inference.
