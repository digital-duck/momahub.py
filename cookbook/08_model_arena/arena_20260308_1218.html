A factorial is a mathematical operation denoted by an exclamation mark (!) following a non-negative integer. It represents the product of all positive integers less than or equal to that number. The symbolism for calculating a factorial can be represented as follows: if `n` is our chosen non-negative integer, then its factorial (often written as `n!`) equals n × (n - 1) × (n - 2) × ... × 3 × 2 × 1. It’s important to note that by convention, the factorial of zero (0!) is defined to be equal to one (1). Here's an example: The calculation of `5!` or "five factorial" would yield a result as follows: - Start with the original number 5. Multiply it downwards by each successive integer less than itself until you reach 1, so we get this sequence - 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120. Thus `5!` equals to 120. - Similarly for calculating the factorial of zero (0!) which by convention is equal to one: since it’s just a single term, we don't need any multiplication and hence result in 1 i.e., `0! = 1`.
